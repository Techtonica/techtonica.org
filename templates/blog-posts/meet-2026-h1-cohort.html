Meet Our 2026 H1 Part-Time Cohort!

We are excited about our 2026 H1 cohort, not only because we are always happy to walk this journey with our participants but because of the strides that this unique part-time cohort has made. The participants worked hard through part I of the program. To learn more about them, please read on.

Adrian Guzman (they/them)

Tell us a little about your life

I was born near Disneyland in Orange County, CA to a single mother. I attended Cal State Dominguez Hills in Los Angeles where I was fortunate enough to study abroad in 2 places, earning a BA in political science. I enjoy petting cats and dogs and listening to crashing waves.

What made you decide to pursue a software engineering career and apply for Techtonica?

I was learning some basic JavaScript on my own but wished I could attend a bootcamp to be able to receive feedback and have more structured learning. A nearby, in-person bootcamp was an option, but that cost about $15,000 and required a full-time commitment. I then ran into a post on LinkedIn about Techtonica, how it was part-time for the first time and free for most people. I immediately applied.

Software engineering as a career is worth pursuing because I really value a high-growth career that rewards learning.

What are you most excited to learn while at Techtonica?

I am very excited to learn about non-technical aspects of software engineering as well as the technical. On the non-technical side, I like being exposed to white-collar-environment methods of communication and self-advocacy. On the technical side, I am excited to learn how to create full-stack apps, as this will showcase a competent level of understanding the fundamentals while also having fun with the creativity in the kinds of apps I make.

What do you want to contribute to or change about the tech industry?

I want to contribute to a tech industry that doesn’t harness or exploit personal data or rely on addiction-inducing algorithms. It hurts to see my relatives and friends become victims of big tech research and development, isolating themselves for ever-increasing screen time and coming to believe the world is far more frightening than it truly is.

What’s your dream project?

It would be cool to work on an app that connects culturally curious strangers in a meaningful way. Maybe an app where you can select the cultures you’re most unfamiliar with and then get paired with a family that belongs to that culture to go to their house and eat dinner together. Maybe the app could also pair people who wish to experience dinner with others who have differing religions, nationalities, politics, sexual identities, and more.

Dari Caceres Castillo (she/her)

Tell us a little about your life

While in my first few months at Techtonica, I graduated from Valencia College with a degree in Computing Technology and Software Development, where I built a strong foundation in software development, collaboration, and problem-solving by working on real-world and academic projects. I contributed to Manila, an open-source OpenStack project for shared file systems supported by Red Hat, where I worked on OpenAPI schemas for file sharing. When I get stuck on a technical problem, an approach that sometimes helps me think the problem through is to explain the concept out loud to my dog, a cocker spaniel named Riley, who patiently tilts his head until I’m done talking. In my free time, I enjoy bouldering, going on long walks with my dog Riley, thrifting, and advocating for accessibility.
What made you decide to pursue a software engineering career and apply for Techtonica?

I decided to pursue a software engineering career because I want to build projects that solve real world problems for customers and create tools or features that they will actually use, and addressing needs they didn’t realize they had. I also really enjoy building projects and sharing them with the world, for anyone to use. I applied to Techtonica because of its cohort-based and community learning approach, where mentors and staff are readily available to support you throughout the program.

What are you most excited to learn while at Techtonica?

I am most excited to grow my software engineering skills through project building and collaborative learning. I am excited about learning best practices for building web applications with my cohort mates while having access to mentors for guidance when I get stuck. I’m especially excited to grow professionally as a software engineer so that I can give back to the community and become a mentor myself.

What do you want to contribute to or change about the tech industry?

I want to contribute to a tech industry that is more accessible for both users and contributors. Software should work for people with different abilities. I’m also interested in building tools that support people in the gig economy, particularly technology that automates the time-consuming process of sourcing, listing, and managing inventory for resellers and small online businesses. Technology should be leveraged to reduce the time spent on repetitive, menial tasks, so that people can reclaim time to pursue other interests and spend more time with their family.

What’s your dream project?

My dream project is to build an application that automates the reselling process, including inventory management, listing creating, cross-listing, sourcing, advertising, and sales tracking. The goal here is to automate the most time-consuming parts of reselling items online or in person. The global secondhand apparel market is growing and is projected to reach $350 billion by 2027, with the U.S. market expected to grow to $73 billion by 2028.

Paige Li (she/her)

Tell us a little about your life

I hold a BSc in Evolutionary Biology and an MSc in Environmental Science and Policy, with a career spanning science, public service, and leadership roles. I am a U.S. Army veteran and have worked with organizations including the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service and the GTM Research Reserve. I am autistic, and these experiences have shaped my perspective and commitment to equity, fairness, and the greater good. I value creativity, collaboration, and building things that serve others. I believe shared meals, shared knowledge, and inclusive communities are foundational to meaningful progress.
What made you decide to pursue a software engineering career and apply for Techtonica?

I chose to pursue software engineering because it allows me to combine problem-solving with creativity in a way that feels similar to making art. My background made me increasingly aware of how technology shapes access and equity. I applied to Techtonica because of its mission-driven focus, inclusive values, and support for people transitioning into tech from nontraditional backgrounds.

What are you most excited to learn while at Techtonica?

I am particularly interested in learning best practices for writing clear, maintainable code and collaborating effectively within engineering teams. I am also eager to better understand the full development lifecycle, from design and implementation to testing and iteration.

What do you want to contribute to or change about the tech industry?

I want to help create space in the tech industry for neurodivergent individuals whose ways of thinking and experiencing the world are often overlooked. Much of the industry is built around assumptions that do not account for sensory differences, communication styles, or processing needs. By advocating for clearer systems, more flexible collaboration, and inclusive design practices, I hope to help shift tech culture toward environments where neurodivergent people can do their best work. Creating that space benefits not only neurodivergent individuals, but the quality and accessibility of the technology itself.

What’s your dream project?

My dream project is an open-source web platform that uses AI to make knowledge more accessible across different learning styles. Users would enter a topic they want to learn and select how they learn best — such as visual, auditory, read/write, or kinesthetic — and receive a structured learning pathway with curated resources tailored to that preference. The platform would invite community contributions (aggregating knowledge in one place) to support an ELI5-style learning. I am also interested in using AI to help experts translate their complex knowledge into accessible ELI5 formats, making learning more inclusive and adaptable for everyone.
