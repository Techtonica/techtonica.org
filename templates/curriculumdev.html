Openings

Techtonica is a nonprofit that offers six months of full-time software engineering training with living stipends to Bay Area women and non-binary adults with low incomes. After graduation, Techtonica places graduates into engineering jobs with sponsor companies. You can read more here.

Make an Impact as Our Curriculum Developer!

Techtonica offers a life-changing learning experience to participants. We care deeply about helping our participants become high-quality developers.

We're looking for a curriculum developer for our six-month, full-time software engineering training. We have existing materials from our first 2018 cohort (see some of the subjects we cover here) that you will build upon and improve. This is a part-time position.

Responsibilities:

  • Decide on the overall plan and pacing for the curriculum, including the balance of lessons, projects, and assessments, while incorporating feedback
  • Develop and improve topic outlines, projects, and assessments
  • Break down necessary work into individual pieces that can be developed by volunteers and manage that development
  • Review work of volunteers for accuracy, completeness, and overall quality

What we're looking for:

  • You can clearly explain technical topics in writing and in a way that is rigorous but can be understood by beginners
  • You know what is needed to become a high-quality, entry-level software engineer and have good ideas about how to get people to that point
  • You have an understanding of socioeconomic differences and experiences that our participants have faced

Preferred previous experience:

  • Developing a technical curriculum
  • Teaching
  • Professional software development
