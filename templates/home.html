What We Do

We're like a coding bootcamp, but our program covers more than just web development, and it's longer, free to students, and for local women and non-binary femme adults in need. Our program also has job placement at the end, and criteria, curriculum, and mentoring support from sponsoring companies. (See what else sets us apart in our FAQs.)

Why

Tech companies in the Bay Area are causing displacement and increasing income disparity. They also need to build more diverse technical teams.

We asked, "What if we could help companies build much-needed diverse technical teams by having them sponsor the training for underrepresented, underprivileged locals who need jobs the most?"

How it Works

Vetting

Techtonica conducts free intro workshops with local organizations and labs.

We invite the best students to apply for the full-time program.

Partner Companies

Partner companies sponsor the training and provide mentors.

They have a say in the curriculum and receive inclusion training in preparation for employing one of their top-rated students at the end of the program.

The sponsorship is tax-deductible.

Training

Accepted students receive living stipends, laptops, childcare if needed, and six months of full-time training that includes lectures, projects, open-source contributions, and experienced guidance.

Upon completion of the program, students are placed with partner companies.

How to Help

  • Encourage your company to sponsor and hire students.
  • Sign up to volunteer.
  • Host the first class in your space for a month.
  • Donate (donations are tax-deductible and you can sign up to donate monthly).
